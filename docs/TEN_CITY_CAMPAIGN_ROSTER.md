# Surveillance Survivor — Ten-City Campaign Roster

## Status

- **Authority:** canonical campaign-content specification
- **Campaign size:** 10 city levels
- **Ordering:** escalating surveillance-network complexity
- **Final trilogy:** New York City → Los Angeles → Atlanta
- **Research qualification:** no complete authoritative national city ranking of Flock deployments exists. This roster is evidence-weighted and gameplay-ordered, not a definitive national ranking.

## Satire and likeness policy

Each level compresses a city's visual language, landmarks, food, infrastructure, civic mythology, and surveillance politics into a readable iPhone-scale battlefield. Enemies should roast institutions, procurement, rhetoric, data-sharing, and techno-solutionism—not ordinary residents.

Bosses default to original fictional composites. Public figures and executives may inform policy satire, but direct likenesses require separate approval and legal review.

## 1. Wichita, Kansas — The Panopticon of the Plains

**Landmarks and environment:** Keeper of the Plains, Arkansas River bridges, aircraft hangars, grain elevators, rail yards, tornado-warning infrastructure, wide suburban arterials.

**Standard enemies**

- **Airframe Auditor:** aviation inspector with clipboard wings; marks the player for aerial tracking.
- **Prairie Plate Scout:** camera pole disguised as wheat; concealed until its lens flashes.
- **HOA Tornado Warden:** covenant enforcer who builds temporary fence corridors.
- **Cessna Citation Drone:** executive-jet scanner performing fast strafing passes.
- **Grain-Silo Spotter:** rolling mini-silo with long-range optics.

**Elite:** **The Zoning Cyclone** — rotating ordinances, cones, maps, and violation notices.

**Boss:** **The Aviation Security Commissioner** — procurement technocrat inside an armored budget binder. Deploys aircraft drones, creates restricted airspace, and converts the arena into a radar screen.

## 2. Louisville, Kentucky — Derby Day Data Dragnet

**Landmarks and environment:** Churchill Downs, Twin Spires, bourbon warehouses, Ohio River waterfront, Victorian brick neighborhoods, baseball-bat factories, blacked-out camera maps.

**Standard enemies**

- **Derby Detail Deputy:** tactical jockey on a motorized barricade.
- **Bourbon Barrel Scanner:** rolling panoramic sensor leaving distortion pools.
- **Hot Brown Informant:** sentient casserole emitting grease hazards.
- **Secret Location Steward:** faceless official who hides nearby cameras.
- **Slugger Compliance Officer:** bat inspector who reflects projectiles.

**Elite:** **The Redaction Thoroughbred** — erases sections of the minimap while charging.

**Boss:** **The Keeper of Confidential Coordinates** — assembled from closed-session doors, NDAs, and blacked-out maps. Makes cameras invisible and redacts the screen.

## 3. Tulsa, Oklahoma — The Petroleum Panopticon

**Landmarks and environment:** Art Deco towers, Route 66 neon, oil derricks, Golden Driller imagery, storm shelters, motel corridors.

**Standard enemies**

- **Deco Detective:** chrome geometric officer with ricocheting search beams.
- **Route 66 Reader:** roadside sign activating as the player crosses lanes.
- **Oilfield Observer:** derrick-legged camera pumping Suspicion from the ground.
- **Sooner Subpoena Rider:** cowboy process server on an electric scooter.
- **Storm-Shelter Snitch:** bunker hatch emerging when the player stops.

**Elite:** **The Open-Records Roughneck** — seals map areas using exemption-shaped tools.

**Boss:** **The Golden Watchman** — monumental chrome mascot with a camera face and derrick spine. Extracts movement history as behavioral crude.

## 4. Dayton, Ohio — Gateway City: Every Camera Counts

**Landmarks and environment:** Wright brothers imagery, Riverscape fountains, industrial corridors, abandoned factories, research labs, neighborhood gateway cameras.

**Standard enemies**

- **Gateway Greeter:** smiling subdivision sign that alerts the network.
- **Wright Watcher:** bicycle-wing drone tracking movement vectors.
- **Milestone Monitor:** civic marker gaining armor over time.
- **Community Camera Captain:** flight-goggled commander buffing sensors.
- **Research Annex Rover:** experimental golf cart firing triangulation pulses.

**Elite:** **The Kitty Hawk Hotlist** — paper-airplane swarm carrying wanted-vehicle alerts.

**Boss:** **The Director of Gateway Optimization** — builds checkpoints, copies prior movement paths, and forces the player through scan gates.

## 5. Oakland, California — The Sanctuary Scanner

**Landmarks and environment:** Port cranes, container yards, Lake Merritt, BART infrastructure, downtown murals, Oakland hills, resistance-history motifs.

**Standard enemies**

- **Port Authority Peacock:** crane-legged sensor that relocates the player.
- **Sanctuary Semanticist:** policy drone alternating between friendly and hostile.
- **BART Fare Oracle:** predictive turnstile creature.
- **Containerized Cop:** surprise guard emerging from freight containers.
- **Lake Merritt Lookout:** floating bird-camera tracking across water.

**Elite:** **The Federal-Request Phantom** — borrows access through allied nodes beyond normal jurisdiction.

**Boss:** **The Contract Renewal Hydra** — each head represents a different temporary rationale: extension, emergency, compliance, procurement, vendor transition, or pilot program.

## 6. San Francisco, California — Fog of Probable Cause

**Landmarks and environment:** Golden Gate Bridge, cable cars, steep streets, Sutro Tower, Victorians, autonomous vehicles, sensor-filled fog.

**Standard enemies**

- **Fog Analytics Cloud:** metadata fog hiding sensors but not their scan cones.
- **Cable-Car Correlator:** rail-mounted predictor of travel routes.
- **Sutro Signal Witch:** tower creature amplifying sensor range.
- **Autonomous Informant:** driverless camera car that follows without attacking.
- **Victorian Vendor:** ornate subscription house spawning premium upgrades.

**Elite:** **The Improper Search Daemon** — appears outside jurisdiction using borrowed credentials.

**Boss:** **The Algorithmic Moderate** — activates Public Safety, Civil Liberties, Temporary Safeguard, and Independent Review phases while every phase expands observation.

## 7. Columbus, Ohio — The Six-Hundred-Eye Statehouse

**Landmarks and environment:** Ohio Statehouse, Scioto Mile, Short North arches, campus imagery, suburban jurisdiction patchwork, public-hearing chambers.

**Standard enemies**

- **Buckeye Bureaucrat:** multi-badged clerk splitting into separate jurisdictions.
- **Statewide Share Bear:** friendly mascot connecting every camera.
- **Hearing Scheduler:** calendar golem delaying abilities.
- **Immigration Query Imp:** search-bar creature marking targets for off-map agencies.
- **Short North Sentinel:** gateway arch creating mandatory scans.

**Elite:** **The Fifteen-Thousand-Query Swarm** — grows whenever the player changes jurisdiction.

**Boss:** **The Mayor of Meaningful Review** — opens public-comment portals, pauses one sharing route while activating another, and repeatedly reschedules oversight.

## 8. New York City — The Five-Borough Omnigaze

**Research classification:** represents NYC's broader integrated surveillance ecology, not a verified top-three municipal Flock deployment.

**Landmarks and environment:** Times Square, subway tunnels, Brooklyn Bridge, scaffolding, Central Park, financial-district canyons, rooftop water towers.

**Standard enemies**

- **Scaffold Sentinel:** hidden sensor extending overhead cover.
- **MTA Metadata Rat:** access-card carrier moving through off-screen tunnels.
- **Times Square Ad-Eye:** billboard converting screen brightness into Suspicion.
- **Congestion Oracle:** toll gantry imposing movement penalties.
- **Bodega Recognition Cat:** harmless-looking counter scanner.
- **Finance District Quant Cop:** attacks predicted future positions.

**Elite:** **The Interagency Pizza Rat** — drags one shared database through every borough, adding datasets as toppings.

**Boss:** **The Five-Borough Data Baron** — five borough heads blame one another before merging into **The Real-Time City**.

## 9. Los Angeles, California — Thirty-Five Hundred Eyes, No One in Charge

**Research classification:** satire focuses on decentralized regional public-private surveillance, not one active municipal contract.

**Landmarks and environment:** Hollywood Boulevard, freeway interchanges, Griffith Observatory, downtown skyline, studio backlots, gated communities, port infrastructure, strip malls.

**Standard enemies**

- **Influencer Informant:** ring-light creature livestreaming location.
- **HOA Auteur:** tactical-beige director reframing camera angles.
- **Freeway Follower:** interchange-shaped pursuit drone.
- **Studio Security Extra:** identical guard arriving in repeated reshoots.
- **Valet Plate Broker:** swaps player identity tags.
- **Wellness Watch Captain:** athleisure patrol applying passive-aggressive debuffs.

**Elite:** **The Content Moderation Helicopter** — combines searchlights, sponsored safety messaging, and automated claims.

**Boss:** **The Decentralized Accountability Producer** — removes visible city cameras while activating private neighborhoods, malls, studios, parking operators, and neighboring jurisdictions. Final attack: **We No Longer Have a Contract**.

## 10. Atlanta, Georgia — Flock's Nest

**Landmarks and environment:** downtown freeway trench, Peachtree streets, airport terminals, BeltLine, Centennial Olympic Park, corporate campuses, HOA labyrinths, film lots, server hives.

**Standard enemies**

- **Peachtree Pattern Matcher:** peach-shaped drone copying movement patterns.
- **Airport Identity Shepherd:** terminal robot sorting players into threat lanes.
- **HOA Hive Worker:** patrol bee sharing alerts instantly.
- **BeltLine Behavioral Cyclist:** moving detection loop.
- **Film-Tax Tactical Extra:** respawns after every cut.
- **Data Center Deacon:** server-rack preacher converting hotlists into followers.
- **Procurement Pastor:** heals sensors through recurring invoices.

**Elite:** **The Nationwide Search Bee** — cybernetic hornet containing connected jurisdictions.

**Mid-boss:** **The Public–Private Partnership Chimera** — municipal police, private HOA, and vendor-platform heads, each claiming the others control the data.

**Final boss:** **The Safety Evangelist** — original surveillance-industry prophet inside a cathedral-sized server hive.

1. **Objective Evidence:** plate projectiles and universal metadata conversion.
2. **One Network:** reconnects all ten cities and defeated bosses.
3. **Eliminate Crime:** Suspicion stops decaying; predictive player duplicates appear.
4. **The Flock:** the cathedral becomes thousands of camera-birds; victory requires severing network links to create the final Blind Spot.

## Boss design doctrine

|---|---|---|---|

| Agency | police and interagency expansion | institutional machine | badges, command centers, jurisdiction cables, hotlists |
